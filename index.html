TECHNICAL CONTENT SAMPLES

Whether I’m creating a feature guide, an installation tutorial, or a demo video, all of the technical content I create requires knowledge of three things: the concept (what am I writing about?), the audience (who am I writing for?), and the goal (what do I want this content to accomplish?). I see these three categories as the pillars of effective technical communication. If just one is missing, the whole piece will go astray. Here are some samples of technical content I’ve produced, explored through the lens of this simple framework.

Forward customer data to Snowflake for analysis and storage

CONCEPT: This tutorial focuses on an mParticle integration with Snowflake that allows customers to forward cleansed and unified customer data from their CDP to their Data Warehouse. This integration relieves data engineers of the need to build a custom data connection between these two systems, and can be implemented by a simple UI-based workflow.

AUDIENCE: This content is primarily intended for data engineers at mParticle’s customer companies who are responsible for integrating the various systems in their data architecture.

GOALS: The primary goal of this piece was to encourage engineers at mParticle’s customer companies to take advantage of this integration, and allow them to use this tutorial alone to implement it. Additionally, the tutorial also aimed to convey the benefits of establishing this data connection through the use case and examples it highlights.
